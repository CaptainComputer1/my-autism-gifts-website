Applying Austin Coldiron's Story Selling to MAG

Sean Lawrence · 2026-04-26 · Source: AUSTIN_COLDIRON_TRANSCRIPT.md + STORYTELLING_PRINCIPLES.md in the cross-project folder
Austin's framework in one breath: get the right people in a room, tell the right four stories (origin · vehicle · internal-belief · external-belief) — each shaped as a hero's journey with a clear epiphany — install one big-domino belief that makes your offer the only logical next step, then transition to the pitch with micro-commitment Yes-questions. Don't sell improvements; sell a new vehicle.

Section A — Site copy rewrites

1. Homepage hero — pick the headline that aligns with Austin's principles

Austin principle applied: hero copy must pass the 5-second test (who is this for, what do they get) AND begin breaking the biggest internal false belief on the first read. The headline is the audience's "ordinary world" → "call to adventure" handoff.

Three candidates already exist in MAG_Copy_Drafts.md. Through Austin's lens:

CandidateHeadlineAustin's verdict
A — Recommended "You Were Never Broken. Let's Prove It." Strongest fit. It's an internal-false-belief breaker on word one ("never broken" = direct rebuttal of the lifelong label). It's the epiphany of the entire hero's journey, compressed into 5 words. Promises proof (the logical hook) and uses the audience's language.
B — Safe "Coaching for Autistic and Neurodivergent Adults — and Anyone Ready to Grow" Passes 5-sec test, but it's a category description, not a story hook. No epiphany. No belief shift. Use only if the audience is so cold that "is this even for me?" is the bigger problem than belief.
C — Personal "I've Been Where You Are. Here's What's Possible." Strong on origin-story signaling and mentor framing. Slightly weaker on epiphany — the belief shift is implied, not stated. Best paired with a sub-headline that names the specific "broken" label being shed.

Recommendation: Candidate A. It IS the epiphany of Rob's whole story compressed.

4. Services page — apply the "new opportunity" / "big domino" framing

Austin principle applied: don't sell improvements (better coaching). Sell a new vehicle. The big domino belief, if your audience accepts it, makes your offer the only logical next step.

Proposed big-domino statement for MAG

Following Austin's template:

The fastest and most effective way to build the life an autistic or neurodivergent adult actually wants is by working with someone who has lived it themselves — and the only place you'll get that, paired with 41 years of personal-development practice, is in coaching with Rob.

Use this statement as the spine of the Services page intro. Every package below it becomes the mechanism for accessing the new opportunity (lived-experience-driven coaching), not just a price tier.

5. "Why These Prices?" objection-handling block

Austin principle applied: external false belief story. The "I can't afford this" objection has to be broken with the same 4-element pattern: old belief → new belief → story → proof.

The current draft (in MAG_Copy_Drafts.md) is good — it covers the new belief (these rates are intentionally accessible) and gestures at proof (specialized coaching typically runs $145-225). To complete the pattern, add ONE specific story:

Add to the existing "Why These Prices?" block One client paused for three weeks before booking — she told me later she kept calculating whether she could "afford it." When she finally did, her first measurable shift came within five sessions: she got her first job interview in two years. The cost wasn't the price. The cost was the three weeks she spent not doing it.
Story breaks the "I can't afford this" belief by reframing the real cost as the delay, not the dollars. Needs Rob's confirmation of a real client whose story matches this shape (or his approval to lightly fictionalize a composite — though composites carry ethical risk and should be flagged as such).

Current form (already in clarity_call.html) follows MAG_Copy_Drafts' 10-question structure: about you → your story → what you're looking for → how we'll work. This is already aligned with the micro-commitment principle.

Two surgical edits to lean further in:

Recommendation: defer the $33/month Kit Creator Plan upgrade. Both the Connect form and the Clarity Call intake form route submissions to Rob's inbox; he responds personally within 24 hours. That personal response IS the nurture sequence. An automated sequence layered on top would either duplicate Rob's voice (weakening it) or add noise. Why this conclusion changes if/when MY-63 launches

A lead magnet (PDF, quiz, or email course) creates a different audience: people who opted in for value but didn't book a call. They're warmer than cold traffic, colder than form submitters. A nurture sequence is exactly what serves that gap. At that point, the $33/mo Kit Creator Plan is justified.
