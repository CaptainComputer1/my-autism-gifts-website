# MAG Brand Rules

> Authored 2026-04-23 after Meeting 8 with Elizabeth and Rob.
> These rules are binding on all creative output for the MAG project.

## Exclusive assets — must not be reused outside MAG

The following assets were created **specifically for Rob Hodes / My Autism Gifts** under a compensation agreement with Elizabeth. They cannot be redistributed, resold, placed on other projects, or handed to third parties.

- **The MAG lettering** (the stylized "MAG" wordmark with the integrated butterfly on the G)
- **The specific butterfly design** that sits on the G of the MAG wordmark
- **Every derivative** that uses the above lettering or butterfly — including the Blue Fortress banner, the Gold Marquee, the Purple Blue-Banner variants, and all Zoom backgrounds built from them

Elizabeth's direct quote from Meeting 8 (2026-04-23):

> "MAG was created with an agreement for me to use with Rob, so it's not something you can hand out to other people. And that specific butterfly same thing — that's why you can't find that lettering anywhere else."

## Brand color discipline

- **Primary palette is fixed.** Do not introduce new brand colors without Elizabeth's sign-off. Elizabeth's framing (Meeting 8): "Does Nike ever change the color unless they're actually emphasizing a new premium product? When they first came out, they always were the same."
- **Primary colors in use:**
  - Navy — `#2A3A56` (primary dark — headings, strong text on light backgrounds)
  - Teal — `#53A0B7` (secondary — accents, links)
  - Deep Purple — `#4A3B63` (accent — primary CTAs)
  - Soft Purple — `#D2CCFF` (MAG purple — card and Zoom-background base)
  - Light Purple — `#F9F5FF` (very-light section background, Zoom base highlights)
  - Light Teal — `#97E7FF` (hero label, highlights)
- **Do not use dark purple or navy as a background behind the MAG lettering** — it makes the wordmark illegible. Soft light purple is the approved default.

## Typography discipline

- **Headings**: Vidaloka (serif, Google Fonts)
- **Body**: Roboto (sans-serif, Google Fonts, weights 300/400/500/700)
- Do not introduce a third font family without Elizabeth's sign-off.

## Seasonal / event branding

- For event-themed work (Great Discovery workshops, seasonal launches, speaking appearances), consistency-of-medium within a season is the rule. A spring workshop's Zoom background, email header, and landing page should visibly share the same treatment so returning attendees recognize it. Elizabeth compared this to Tony Robbins' mindfulness-event visuals all sharing a coherent look.
- The **Blue Fortress** banner variant is reserved for event contexts. It is not for regular coaching sessions.

## Content rules for Rob-facing marketing surfaces (Zoom, social, website)

- Keep visible text minimal. Website URL under a name is acceptable; email addresses, phone numbers, and long taglines on Zoom backgrounds are not.
- Elizabeth (Meeting 8): "They need you. People will Google you. They don't need a whole bunch of stuff on the Zoom screen."

## Change-control

- Any new brand asset (background variant, logo derivative, color introduction) requires Elizabeth's written approval before use.
- The brand agreement between Rob and Elizabeth is prior art; this document only records the rules, not the agreement terms.
